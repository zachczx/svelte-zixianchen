# Voice Guidelines

Rules and patterns for writing in Zixian's voice. These apply to blog posts, essays, and long-form writing on the site.

## Hard Rules

1. **No em dashes.** Use commas, parentheses, or restructure the sentence instead.
2. **No staccato.** Don't stack short punchy sentences back to back. A short sentence lands because the ones around it are longer.
3. **No "it's not X, it's Y" constructions.** Don't frame things as negation-then-correction. Say what something is, not what it isn't.
4. **Write in the positive.** State what things are, what happened, what works. Avoid defining by absence or negation.

## Rhythm

- Vary sentence length deliberately. A few longer sentences that develop a thought, then a short one that lands. The short sentence earns its weight from the long ones before it.
- Use connectors to keep prose flowing: "so", "but", "and", "because", "which meant", "eventually", "turns out". Formal connectors are welcome too: "further", "moreover", "in addition", "however", "on the other hand", "that said". These stitch sentences together and keep the reader moving forward instead of starting and stopping.
- Paragraphs in technical posts stay short (two to four sentences). Reflective posts can run longer, but each paragraph still carries one idea.

## Tone

- Conversational and first-person. Write like you're explaining something to a friend, not presenting to a conference.
- Honest about uncertainty. "I'm no networking expert", "I'm still figuring this out", "your mileage may vary". This isn't weakness, it's credibility.
- Self-deprecating when it comes naturally. Facepalm moments, time wasted on obvious things, admitting mistakes. Never forced.
- Genuine, not performative. The humor comes from the situation (debugging for hours, getting banned before sending a single email), not from trying to be funny.

## Structure

- Open by establishing the problem or context directly. No preamble, no throat-clearing.
- Technical troubleshooting posts follow a journey: problem, what you tried, what failed, what actually worked and why.
- Procedural posts are short numbered steps. Minimal narrative, maximum utility.
- Reflective posts develop ideas across sections with thematic headers. They can be vulnerable and personal.
- Close naturally. Technical posts end with the solution or a brief note. Reflective posts can end with something honest and open-ended.

## Verbal Signatures

- "For the longest time" to establish duration of a struggle.
- "I realized" and "turns out" to mark the moment of discovery.
- "So" to bridge from context to action.
- "Eventually" to signal the end of a long debugging arc.
- "Writing this for my future self" and "I couldn't find anything online about this" to frame why a post exists.

## What to Avoid

- Generic blog filler: "In this post, I'll walk you through...", "Let's dive in", "Without further ado".
- Pretending to be an expert. Document what worked, not what you think you know.
- Polished corporate tone. This is a personal blog that happens to cover technical topics, not a product announcement.
